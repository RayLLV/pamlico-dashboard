Pamlico Capital History & Milestones
1988
Founded as First Union Capital Partners — proprietary investment arm of First Union National Bank in Charlotte, NC. One of the earliest bank-affiliated PE platforms.
2001
First Union merges with Wachovia Corporation. Capital Partners group continues operating under the new Wachovia umbrella.
2002
Raises first dedicated institutional fund (PC I, ~$950M, raised as Wachovia Capital Partners). Begins investing third-party LP capital alongside bank capital for the first time.
2008
Wells Fargo acquires Wachovia during financial crisis. The PE team navigates complex integration while maintaining investment pace.
2010
Management buyout from Wells Fargo — rebrands as Pamlico Capital. Named after the Pamlico Sound on the NC Outer Banks. Ridgemont similarly spins out of Bank of America the same year.
2016
Raises PC IV ($910M), marking significant fundraising step-up. Deepens focus on communications, healthcare IT, and software sectors.
2020
Raises PC V at $1.4B hard cap, crossing the billion-dollar fund threshold for the first time. Five consecutive GrowthCap Top Growth Equity Firm awards.
2025
Closes PC VI at $1.75B — 25% step-up from PC V. Surpasses $6.5B+ total AUM. Continues pattern of exceeding fundraising targets.
